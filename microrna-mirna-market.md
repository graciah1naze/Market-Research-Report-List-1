<p><p>MicroRNA (miRNA) is a class of small, non-coding RNA molecules that play a crucial role in regulating gene expression. These molecules are typically 20 to 24 nucleotides long and function by binding to complementary sequences on messenger RNA (mRNA), leading to mRNA degradation or inhibition of translation. MiRNAs are involved in various biological processes, including development, cell differentiation, growth, and apoptosis, making them significant in both normal physiological functions and disease pathways.</p><p>The MicroRNA (miRNA) Market is witnessing robust growth, driven by the increasing emphasis on precision medicine, advancements in genomic research, and the escalating prevalence of chronic diseases.
