<p><p>The Gigabit Interface Converter (GBIC) is a transceiver used to connect gigabit Ethernet networks to various types of fiber optic cables. The market is categorized into two primary types: single-mode GBIC and multimode GBIC. Single-mode GBIC is designed for long-distance communication, utilizing a narrow beam of light through a single fiber strand, while multimode GBIC supports shorter distances, transmitting light through multiple pathways within a thicker fiber cable. Both types are essential for optimizing data transmission in different network environments.</p></p>
